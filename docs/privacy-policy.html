Resistor — Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 10, 2026

What Resistor Collects

Resistor stores the data you create within the app:

  • Habit names, descriptions, colors, and icons
  • Temptation events: timestamps, outcomes, intensity levels, context tags, and notes
  • App preferences: accent color, default habit, context prompt setting

All of this data is created by you and stored on your device.

iCloud Sync

If you are signed in to iCloud, Resistor syncs your data across your devices using Apple's CloudKit. Your data is stored in your private iCloud account and is not accessible to the developer or any third party. Apple's iCloud terms and privacy policy apply to this storage.

No Analytics or Tracking

Resistor does not collect analytics, crash reports, usage metrics, or any telemetry. There are no third-party SDKs, advertising frameworks, or tracking pixels in the app.

No Third-Party Sharing

Your data is never shared with, sold to, or accessed by any third party. The developer does not have access to your data.

No Account Required

Resistor does not require you to create an account or provide any personal information. iCloud handles identity through your Apple ID.

Data Export and Deletion

You can export all of your data as a JSON file at any time from the Habits screen. You can also delete all data from within the app. Deleting the app removes all local data. iCloud data can be managed through your device's iCloud storage settings.

In-App Purchases

Resistor offers an optional tip jar. Purchases are processed by Apple through the App Store. The developer does not collect or store any payment information.

Children's Privacy

Resistor is not directed at children under 13. The app does not knowingly collect data from children.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted here with a new date. Material changes will be noted in the app's release notes.

Contact

Questions about this policy can be directed to the developer through the App Store listing.
